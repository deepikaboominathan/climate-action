Why Climate Action?

Climate action is crucial to mitigate the adverse effects of climate change, which threaten ecosystems, economies, and human health. By reducing greenhouse gas emissions and promoting sustainable practices, we can prevent extreme weather events, protect biodiversity, and ensure a habitable planet for future generations. Immediate and collective efforts are essential to safeguard our environment and promote global stability.Climate change refers to significant changes in global temperatures and weather patterns over time. While climate change is a natural phenomenon, human activities have accelerated its pace dramatically since the 1800s. The primary driver is the increase in greenhouse gases, such as carbon dioxide and methane, due to activities like burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes. These gases trap heat in the atmosphere, leading to a greenhouse effect that raises Earth's average temperatures. The impacts of climate change are profound, including more frequent and severe weather events, rising sea levels, and melting glaciers and ice caps. Understanding these processes is crucial for mitigating adverse effects and adapting to changes that are already occurring.
