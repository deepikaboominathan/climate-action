Use Clean Energy

Select a clean, renewable energy source. ... Switch all lighting to LED light bulbs. ... Weatherize your home or business. ... Upgrade to efficient electric appliances. ... Make the switch to an electric vehicle. ... Replace your HVAC with a heat pump. ... Look into a solar installation.


Homeowners and renters can use clean energy at home by buying green power, installing renewable energy systems to generate electricity, or using renewable resources for water and space heating and cooling. Before installing a renewable energy system, it's important to reduce your energy consumption and improve your home energy efficiency Visit Energy Saver to learn more about the use of renewable energy at home You may be eligible for federal and state tax credits if you install a renewable energy system in your home. Visit ENERGY STAR to learn about federal renewable energy tax credits for homeowners. For information on state incentives, visit the Database of State Incentives for Renewables and Efficiency
